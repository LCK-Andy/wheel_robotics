### **2.3 Airspeed, Wind Speed, and Ground Speed**

When modeling the motion of a Micro Air Vehicle (MAV), it’s essential to distinguish between **ground speed** (motion with respect to the earth) and **airspeed** (motion with respect to the surrounding air).  

- **Ground velocity** is the MAV’s velocity relative to the **inertial (earth-fixed) frame**.  
- **Airspeed** is the MAV’s velocity relative to the **air mass** (which itself may be moving due to wind).  

These are related by:

\[
\mathbf{V}_{\text{ground}} = \mathbf{V}_{\text{airspeed}} + \mathbf{V}_{\text{wind}}
\tag{2.5}
\]

where  
- \(\mathbf{V}_{\text{ground}}\) → velocity relative to Earth (what GPS measures),  
- \(\mathbf{V}_{\text{wind}}\) → wind velocity (air motion relative to the Earth),  
- \(\mathbf{V}_{\text{airspeed}}\) → velocity of MAV relative to the air.

---

### **Velocity Components in the Body Frame**

Let the MAV body axes be denoted by \((x_b, y_b, z_b)\), corresponding roughly to (forward, right, down). The **ground velocity** in the body frame is:

\[
\mathbf{V}_b =
\begin{bmatrix}
u \\ v \\ w
\end{bmatrix}
\]

where
- \(u\), \(v\), \(w\) are the body-frame components of the ground velocity.

The **wind velocity** (originally given in North–East–Down coordinates as \(V_{N_w}, V_{E_w}, V_{D_w}\)) can also be expressed in the body frame using a rotation matrix \(\mathcal{R}_{b}^{n}\):

\[
\mathbf{V}_{w,b} = \mathcal{R}_{b}^{n}
\begin{bmatrix}
V_{N_w} \\ V_{E_w} \\ V_{D_w}
\end{bmatrix}
\]

---

### **Airspeed Vector in the Body Frame**

Since the **airspeed** is the velocity relative to the wind, in the **wind frame** it is simply:

\[
\mathbf{V}_{a,w} =
\begin{bmatrix}
V_a \\ 0 \\ 0
\end{bmatrix}
\]

where \(V_a\) is the **airspeed magnitude**.  
Transforming this vector to the **body frame** gives:

\[
\mathbf{V}_{a,b} =
\begin{bmatrix}
u_a \\ v_a \\ w_a
\end{bmatrix}
=
\begin{bmatrix}
V_a \cos\alpha \cos\beta \\
V_a \sin\beta \\
V_a \sin\alpha \cos\beta
\end{bmatrix}
\tag{2.6}
\]

where  
- \(\alpha\) = **angle of attack** (pitch angle between the body x-axis and the relative wind),  
- \(\beta\) = **sideslip angle** (angle between projection of relative wind on the xy-plane and the x-axis).

---

### **Inverse Relations**

From the above relationships, we can extract the aerodynamic angles and airspeed magnitude:

\[
V_a = \sqrt{u_a^2 + v_a^2 + w_a^2}
\]

\[
\alpha = \tan^{-1}\left(\frac{w_a}{u_a}\right)
\]

\[
\beta = \sin^{-1}\left(\frac{v_a}{V_a}\right)
\tag{2.7}
\]

---

### **Summary and Use**

In flight dynamics simulations:

- The **body-frame ground velocity components** \((u, v, w)\) are **state variables** solved from the equations of motion.  
- The **wind components** (from a wind model) are **inputs**.  
- Combining these allows one to compute the **airspeed components** \((u_a, v_a, w_a)\), which determine the **aerodynamic forces and moments** acting on the MAV.
